Writing Experience

I have an extensive experience in technical and creative writing targeted at a wide range of audiences, both in English and in French: I have written scientific papers, reviews, and grant applications, designed an infographic and courses, made a business model, and I also have a personal website for my creative writing. Several of those were long-term projects made with the contribution of interdisciplinary teams.

Grant applications

I have written several grant applications that were submitted to universities' bodies, governmental organisations, and charities. For this, I had to communicate highly technical information so that it was understandable to scientists specialised in these fields, non-specialist scientists, and lay audience.

  • £7,386 in 2019 for a bridging fellowship from the University of St Andrews (UK).
  • £680 in 2017 from the University of St Andrews to go to the European Society of Cardiology Basic Science Summer School in Nice (France).

Infographic

In 2016, I designed an infographic to present the Stewart lab to the general public. This was part of a Wellcome Trust project to create a leaflet introducing the research groups of the BSRC (Biomedical Sciences Research Complex, University of St Andrews, UK).

Course design

In 2017, as part of an outreached organised between the University of St Andrews and the local community, I designed workshops teaching academic skills (how to study efficiently, how to organise your schedule, studies and homework...) to 16-17 years old. I then taught them at Madras College, St Andrews (UK).

In 2018, as part of evening classes delivered by the Open Association of the University of St Andrews, I have also designed two 2-hours seminars about 'Metal Micronutrients' and 'Mitochondria' and taught them to members of the local community.

Business model

In 2012, I participated in Campus Creation, which was a one-year group competition in Lyon (France), in which we had to create a company and its product or service and write a business model. We then presented our project to investors and professionals from the financial sector. Our project was the company Héméra, which helped families clean a deceased relative' house.

Our team won the prize 'social performance in companies' delivered by APICIL, a French insurance company.
